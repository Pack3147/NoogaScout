Who Are the Cub Scouts?

Cub Scouts is a program within Scouting America, designed for boys and girls in kindergarten through fifth grade. It's a year-round program that provides fun, adventure, and challenges for young children while teaching them core values, leadership skills, and self-reliance.

Cub Scouts are organized into dens by grade level and gender, and they participate in various activities such as camping, hiking, service projects, and earning badges. The program is family-oriented, encouraging parents to be involved in their children's Scouting experience.

What Do Cub Scouts Do?

Cub Scouts participate in a wide variety of activities that build character, citizenship, and personal fitness. These activities include:

  • Outdoor adventures like camping, hiking, and fishing
  • Service projects that help the community
  • Learning new skills such as first aid, knots, and navigation
  • Working together on team-building activities and games
  • Earning badges and advancements that recognize their achievements

Cub Scout Rank Structure

Each year, Cub Scouts work towards rank advancements that correspond to their grade level. The ranks include:

  • Lion (Kindergarten)
  • Tiger (1st Grade)
  • Wolf (2nd Grade)
  • Bear (3rd Grade)
  • Webelos (4th Grade)
  • Arrow of Light (5th Grade)
